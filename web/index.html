Introduction

Silicon Valley is an area in northern California, southwest of San Francisco in the Santa Clara valley region, so called from the silicon wafers employed in the many high-technology design and manufacturing companies in the semiconductor industry

Since its beginnings decades ago, Silicon Valley has profoundly impacted the world economy and the technology that emerged from the area changed the way many people live. While the term was coined to describe a cluster of semiconductor related companies, it is now often used synonymously as a term that describes tech-based startup entrepreneurship in general.

The GDP generated by the region dwarfs that of many countries, and it remains a top choice of location for entrepreneurs to seek funding. Through our project, we want to take you through a visual history of this impressive Rise of the Silicon Valley as a significant hub of startups and a major economic powerhouse.

Data

We acquire financial data on Silicon Vally companies from Bloomberg, Bureau of Economica Analysis, Federal Reserve Bank, and Kaggle. The information contained within our project is publicly available

We define Silicon Valley with six counties in Northern California: Santa Clara, San Mateo, Alameda, Santa Cruz, San Francisco. These six counties comprise of 34 cities. Detailed information on our data and code can be found on our group public repository.
